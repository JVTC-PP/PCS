SEC-01

Program Overview

Mission

What Is the DP3 Program?

The Defense Personal Property Program (DP3) is the DoW's comprehensive system for managing the shipment and storage of household goods (HHG) for active duty military, Reserve/National Guard members, and eligible civilian employees during Permanent Change of Station (PCS), separation, and retirement.

Managed by Department of War Personal Property Activity (DoW PPA), DP3 connects service members with approved Transportation Service Providers (TSPs) through the Defense Personal Property System (DPS) — an online portal for scheduling, tracking, and managing all shipment activity.

Entitlements

What Are You Entitled To?

  • Household Goods (HHG) — Government-arranged full-service move based on rank/grade and dependency status
  • Personally Procured Move (PPM/DITY) — Self-move with incentive payment up to 100% of Government Constructive Cost
  • Unaccompanied Baggage (UB) — Separate air-expedited shipment for essential items needed immediately
  • Non-Temporary Storage (NTS) — Long-term storage during overseas tours and certain assignments
  • Storage-in-Transit (SIT) — Up to 90 days temporary storage at origin or destination
  • Pro-Gear — Additional weight allowance for professional books, papers, and equipment
Move Types

Types of Shipments

  • Full-Service Move — TSP packs, loads, transports, and delivers your goods
  • Partial PPM — Move some items yourself; TSP handles the rest
  • Full PPM — You arrange and complete the entire move yourself
  • CONUS moves — Continental U.S. shipments via ground or containerized transport
  • OCONUS moves — Overseas moves via authorized international TSPs
  • Express Shipments — Rapid delivery option for select items when needed quickly
Your Responsibilities

Key Service Member Actions

  • Contact your TMO/PPO as soon as orders are received
  • Create a DPS account and submit move requests online
  • Inventory high-value items and document with photos/video before packing
  • Be present or have a designated representative for pickup and delivery
  • Annotate exceptions on the inventory at delivery — never sign a blank form
  • File loss/damage claims within 9 months of delivery
  • File inconvenience claims if TSP misses pickup or delivery window

Tip · Claims

Document Everything

Before pack-out, photograph and video every item of value. Create a written inventory. This evidence is essential for claims and significantly speeds up TSP reimbursement.

Tip · Delivery

Never Sign Blank

At delivery, always annotate any damage or missing items on the DD Form 1840 before signing. Signing a clean form waives your ability to dispute those items later.

Tip · PPM

Get Weight Tickets

For PPM moves, obtain certified weight tickets before and after loading at a certified scale. These are required to claim your incentive payment from the government.
